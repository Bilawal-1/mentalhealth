🛡️ Mental Health Parity Act (MHPAEA)

Federal law requires insurance plans to cover mental health and substance use services no more restrictively than medical/surgical benefits. This means:

  • Same copays and deductibles as physical health
  • Same prior authorization standards
  • Same network adequacy requirements
  • You can appeal any denial citing parity violations

📋 Understanding Your EOB

Your Explanation of Benefits (EOB) shows what your insurer paid and what you owe. Key things to check:

  • Billed amount vs. allowed amount
  • Deductible applied — has it been met?
  • Copay/coinsurance your responsibility
  • Denial reason code — always check these
  • Provider contracted vs. out-of-network

🔄 How to Appeal a Denial

Denied claims can almost always be appealed. Steps:

  • Request the denial reason in writing
  • Get a letter of medical necessity from your provider
  • File an internal appeal within the plan deadline (usually 180 days)
  • Request a peer-to-peer review if denied again
  • File an external appeal with your state insurance commissioner

📱 Telehealth & Mental Health Coverage

Since 2020, telehealth mental health coverage expanded significantly. Most commercial and Medicare plans now cover video sessions. Audio-only (phone) coverage varies by payer. Always confirm your plan covers telehealth before the session.

🏥 Finding In-Network Providers

Use your insurer's online provider directory and always verify by calling the provider directly. Ask: Are you contracted with my plan? Are you accepting new patients? What is my cost per session? Do you bill insurance directly?

💰 Out-of-Pocket Maximum

Once you hit your annual out-of-pocket maximum, your insurance pays 100% of covered in-network services for the rest of the year. Track your accumulator — it resets January 1. For 2025 the ACA cap is $9,200 individual / $18,400 family.

For Providers

Maximize Revenue, Minimize Risk

Evidence-based billing, documentation, and compliance strategies for psychiatrists, psychologists, LCSWs, NPs, and counselors.

📝 Documentation Must-Haves

For every psychotherapy session, your note must include:

  • Start time and end time (for time-based codes)
  • DSM-5 diagnosis with ICD-10 code (specific, not unspecified)
  • Chief complaint and mental status examination
  • Modality of therapy (CBT, DBT, etc.)
  • Response to treatment and progress toward goals
  • Risk assessment (suicidality/homicidality)
  • Plan for next session

💻 Telehealth Billing Rules (2025)

Key rules for compliant telehealth billing:

  • Use POS 10 when patient is at home (post-2022)
  • Use Modifier 95 for synchronous video — required by most payers
  • Use Modifier 93 for audio-only where allowed
  • Confirm patient is in a state where you are licensed
  • Document patient location at start of session
  • Obtain patient consent for telehealth

💊 E/M vs Psychotherapy

Psychiatric providers can bill both on the same day using add-on codes:

  • Bill 99213–99215 for medication management (E/M)
  • Add +90833 (16–37 min), +90836 (38–52 min), or +90838 (53+ min)
  • Apply Modifier 25 to the E/M code
  • Document both the E/M and psychotherapy separately in the note
  • Significant time savings vs billing 90837 alone

🏥 Payer-Specific Rules

Major payer policies for mental health:

  • Medicare: No 90837 daily limit; requires UB-04 for facility settings
  • Medicaid: Varies by state — check your state MCO contract
  • BCBS: Requires pre-auth for 90791; 16-session limit varies by plan
  • Aetna: Behavioral health carved out in many plans — verify
  • UHC: Requires Optum credentialing separate from UHC

📋 Credentialing Checklist

  • NPI Type 1 (individual) and Type 2 (group/entity)
  • CAQH ProView profile — keep it updated quarterly
  • State license + DEA (if applicable)
  • Malpractice insurance certificate
  • W-9 and voided check (EFT setup)
  • CV / work history (10 years)
  • Board certifications
  • Hospital affiliations (if applicable)
  • Credentialing takes 90–180 days — apply early

💰 Fee Schedule Optimization

Common underpricing mistakes:

  • Billing 90837 below Medicare rate ($171.35 in 2025)
  • Not adjusting for geographic locality (GPCIs)
  • Using billed charges = allowed charges (leave money on table)
  • Not negotiating commercial rates above Medicare
  • Forgetting to update charges annually for Medicare rate changes

For Billers

From Clean Claim to Zero AR

Proven billing workflows, denial strategies, and appeal tools built specifically for mental health and behavioral health billing.

✅ Pre-Submission Clean Claim Checklist

  • Patient demographics verified against insurance card
  • Insurance eligibility checked (same-day or day before)
  • NPI on claim matches credentialed NPI
  • Correct POS code (11 office / 10 telehealth)
  • ICD-10 code specific (not F90.9 when F90.0/1/2 applicable)
  • CPT code appropriate for documented time
  • Modifier applied where required (25, 95, 59)
  • Authorization number on claim if required
  • Rendering and billing provider NPIs both present
  • Date of service matches documentation

🚫 Top 15 Denial Reasons + Fixes

  • CO-4: Incorrect procedure modifier → Add/correct modifier
  • CO-11: Diagnosis inconsistent with procedure → Fix ICD-10 crosswalk
  • CO-22: Coordination of benefits → Bill primary first, submit COB
  • CO-27: Expenses incurred after coverage ended → Verify eligibility
  • CO-29: Timely filing exceeded → Appeal with proof of timely submission
  • CO-45: Charge exceeds fee schedule → Adjust to contracted rate
  • CO-96: Non-covered charge → Verify benefit and obtain ABN if Medicare
  • CO-97: Bundled service → Unbundle per NCCI or add Modifier 59
  • PR-1: Deductible not met → Bill patient
  • PR-2: Copay → Bill patient
  • OA-23: PA not obtained → Retroactive PA or write off
  • MA-130: Missing/incorrect info → Correct and resubmit
  • N-417: Not credentialed → Expedite credentialing, consider locum
  • B-15: Provider not in network → Out-of-network appeal or single-case agreement
  • N-240: Timely filing second payer → Submit within 90 days of primary EOB

📈 AR Management Strategy

30 Days: Review all unpaid claims. Run eligibility retroactively. Call payer if claim not in system.

60 Days: File appeals for all denied claims. Escalate to provider relations. Document all contacts.

90 Days: Peer-to-peer review for high-value claims. Second-level internal appeals.

120+ Days: External appeal with state insurance commissioner. Consider state attorney general complaint for parity violations. Last resort: patient advocacy or balance billing (state-dependent).

KPI Targets: Clean claim rate >94% · AR >90 days <15% of total · Denial rate <5% · Days in AR <35

🏦 Secondary Insurance Billing

  • Always bill primary first — wait for EOB before billing secondary
  • Submit secondary within plan's timely filing limit (usually 90–180 days from primary EOB)
  • Include primary EOB with secondary claim
  • Do not re-bill full billed charges — show primary paid amount
  • COB rules: primary pays first, secondary fills the gap up to its benefit
  • Medicare as secondary: submit MSP questionnaire first
  • Medicaid is always payer of last resort

💳 Patient Collections Best Practices

  • Collect copays at time of service — not after the fact
  • Verify benefits and estimate cost before first appointment
  • Use a financial responsibility agreement signed at intake
  • Send statements within 30 days of patient balance creation
  • Offer payment plans for balances over $200
  • Never waive Medicare copays routinely (federal fraud)
  • Sliding fee scale for self-pay patients — document financial hardship

For Coders

Accurate Coding = Maximum Revenue

Psychiatric coding rules, crosswalks, and compliance guidance to prevent audits and capture every billable dollar.

📏 Time-Based Coding Rules

For psychotherapy codes (90832/90834/90837), the AMA uses the "face-to-face time" rule:

  • 16–37 min → 90832 (or +90833 with E/M)
  • 38–52 min → 90834 (or +90836 with E/M)
  • 53+ min → 90837 (or +90838 with E/M)
  • Document exact start and end time in every note
  • Use the midpoint rule: bill the higher code when >50% of range completed
  • Telehealth: same time rules apply, same codes
  • Never round up without documentation support

🔗 CPT + ICD-10 Crosswalk (Key Pairs)

  • 90837 + F41.1 → Psychotherapy for GAD ✅
  • 90837 + F32.1 → Psychotherapy for MDD ✅
  • 90791 + F20.9 → Psych eval for schizophrenia ✅
  • 99214 + F90.2 + +90836 → Med mgmt + therapy for ADHD ✅
  • 90839 + F43.10 → Crisis therapy for PTSD ✅
  • 90853 + F41.9 → Group therapy for anxiety ✅
  • 90847 + F60.3 → Family therapy for BPD ✅
  • 99215 + F31.81 + +90838 → High E/M + therapy for Bipolar II ✅

🚨 Audit Red Flags — Avoid These

  • Billing 90837 for every patient every time (statistically unlikely)
  • Using F-codes ending in .9 (unspecified) routinely — be specific
  • Same note text copied forward session to session (cloned notes)
  • Modifier 25 on every claim without documented E/M
  • Upcoding E/M (99215 when 99213 is appropriate)
  • Billing group therapy (90853) with <2 unrelated patients (Medicare)
  • Missing time documentation for time-based codes
  • Billing 90791 more than 2–3 times per patient per year
  • Unbundling codes that should be billed together

📦 NCCI Bundling Rules

  • 90837 and 99213 cannot be billed together without Modifier 25 on the E/M
  • 90832/90834/90837 cannot be billed with 90833/90836/90838 (mutually exclusive)
  • 90839 and 90840 require same session documentation
  • 90846 and 90847 cannot be billed on the same day for the same family
  • Psychological testing codes (96130–96139) have specific component rules
  • Use the CMS NCCI Policy Manual as your authoritative source

🧪 Psychological Testing Codes

  • 96130: Psych testing evaluation, first hour (physician/QHP)
  • 96131: Psych testing evaluation, each additional hour
  • 96132: Neuropsych testing evaluation, first hour
  • 96133: Neuropsych testing evaluation, add'l hour
  • 96136: Test administration by tech, first 30 min
  • 96137: Test administration by tech, each add'l 30 min
  • Always document test names, scores, and interpretation

📋 Medical Necessity Language

Payers audit for these key phrases in documentation:

  • "Patient continues to meet diagnostic criteria for [DSM-5 diagnosis]"
  • "Treatment is necessary to prevent deterioration/hospitalization"
  • "Patient demonstrates [specific symptoms] that impair [function]"
  • "Response to previous treatment: [partial/none/good]"
  • "Current GAF/functional impairment: [specific]"
  • "Goals for this encounter: [specific measurable goals]"
